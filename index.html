  Artist's Statement/ Biography  
 

 

I am an Ayrshire painter and sculptress who works in Oils, Clay and Bronze. I am classically trained in painting portraiture and in sculpting three-dimensional clay portrait heads, either to be fired or cast in bronze.  In the 1980s I studied portraiture painting under Trevor Makinson of the Glasgow School of Art over a period of two years.  I also attended the Glasgow School of Art one day a week as a part-time student over a period of two years.  My interest in more recent years has spread to Still Life/Florals and these are now my passion.  I like to paint life-size and to create images which portray subjects of great integral beauty in their own right.  I love to paint from life in “true” colours and, through the play of light on any subject, to capture the 3rd dimension.

I am an Artist Member of the Glasgow Society of Women Artists. I am an Artist Member and former President of the Ayr Sketch Club.  I exhibit in Scotland and my paintings feature in collections throughout the UK, France, the USA, Australia, New Zealand and Malaysia.  I am inspired by the Dutch Flower Painters, the Pre-Raphaelites, especially William Waterhouse and Holman Hunt.  I admire the portraits of Sir Henry Raeburn and Sir James Guthrie.  My contemporary influences are David Leffel, Scott Christiansen, Clyde Aspevig and Daniel Keys.
